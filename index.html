FriendlySplat

Train fast. Inspect live. Edit effortlessly.

TL;DR: FriendlySplat is a user-friendly, open-source Gaussian Splatting toolkit, integrating SOTA features

into a unified platform for training, pruning, meshing and segmentation.

Why FriendlySplat

Built for the parts of 3DGS research that usually stay fragmented

FriendlySplat is organized around short experiment cycles: get a scene training quickly, attach geometry priors, inspect what the optimizer is doing, and evaluate pruning or segmentation ideas without rebuilding the whole stack each time.

Faster iteration

Designed for short turnaround runs so scene fitting and ablations are easier to repeat.

Better geometry

Supports geometry-aware constraints so training is not limited to pure photometric fitting.

Clearer monitoring

Live metrics, camera frustums, and viewer feedback make failures easier to diagnose.

Research on sparsity

Pruning, compression, and segmentation experiments fit into the same training loop.

Training Framework

A simple pipeline with room for optional modules

The framework is meant to stay readable: feed in multi-view observations and optional priors, optimize a Gaussian scene with interchangeable training-time components, and emit assets that are useful beyond a final render.

Input

Images, masks, and geometry hints

Multi-view RGB observations can be paired with segmentation masks or geometry priors before optimization begins.

Train

Base 3DGS with pluggable modules

Regularizers, pruning policies, and viewer hooks can be added without changing the overall workflow.

Output

Renders, meshes, metrics, and inspection tools

The same run can produce visual results, extracted geometry, real-time statistics, and debugging views.

Core Features

User-facing capabilities, not just internal modules

Geometry-Aware Regularization

Encourages reconstructions that respect scene structure instead of only chasing image loss.

Useful when you want mesh quality and geometry consistency to matter during training.

Fast Scene Training

Targets short experiment loops so scene-scale iterations stay practical.

Good for rapid ablations, lightweight settings, and quick sanity checks.

Live Viewer and Metrics

Monitor curves, camera frustums, and scene state while training is still running.

Makes it easier to spot unstable runs before wasting a full experiment cycle.

Pluggable Pruning Strategies

Swap in different pruning ideas to study sparsity, quality retention, and compression tradeoffs.

The framework is positioned as a practical place to benchmark pruning research.

Mesh Extraction Pipeline

Turn trained splats into geometry artifacts that are easier to inspect and evaluate.

Pairs well with geometry priors when you want a scene representation beyond render-only output.
